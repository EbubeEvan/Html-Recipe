My Favourite Meal

The name of the meal is Akpu and Onugbu soup

Onugbu soup is originaaly from Anambra state, Nigeria. It is made using bitterleaf

Ingredients List

Ingredient Measurements
Meat 1 kg
Bitterleaf 1 cup
Cocoyam 3-4
Smoked Fish 2
Stockfish 2-3
Ground Crayfish 3 Tablespoons
Ogiri Okpei 1 Tablespoon
Ground dry Pepper 3 Tablespoon
Bullion Cubes 2

Preparation

  1. Start by boiling your meats, start with the tougher meats like Cow leg and Shaki first. When they're slightly tender, add beef or other softer meats. When all the meats are tender, add the Stockfish, leave to cook till soft.
  2. While the meats are boiling, boil the Cocoyam with the skin on. Do not add salt, boil till tender, this should take roughly 20 minutes on medium heat.
  3. When they are soft, gently peel the skin off and pound till smooth in a mortar. Alternatively, chop them into small chunks and pur√©e in your electric blender or food processor till smooth, then set aside.
  4. Wash the Bitter leaves with hot water to get rid of as much bitterness as you can. There should be almost no hint of bitterness left in the leaves after washing. If you're using dried Bitter leaves, you'll need to soak in warm water to plump it up and also get rid of dirt.
  5. When the Meats and Fish are all soft, add Crayfish, smoked Prawns, then add the ground or blended pepper. Stir and combine.
  6. Add Palm oil, leave to thoroughly combine for 3-4 minutes.
  7. Now, turn the heat done to low, add the blended Cocoyam into the stock in small dollops, be careful not to add too much. The consistency should be semi fluid.
  8. The Cocoyam will dissolve; thickening the soul, leave to dissolve and combine for 2-3 minutes. Then add the Ogiri, stir and combine. Taste for seasoning and adjust if necessary.
  9. Then add the washed Bitter Leaves, leave to cook for 2-3 minutes. Do not over cook your vegetables.
  10. Serve with Akpu.
